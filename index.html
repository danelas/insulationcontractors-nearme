From open-cell spray foam to dense-pack cellulose, our contractors handle residential, commercial, and industrial projects.

Spray Foam Insulation

Open & closed-cell polyurethane that air-seals and insulates in one application. Ideal for attics, rim joists, and metal buildings.

  • R-6 to R-7 per inch (closed-cell)
  • Air & vapor barrier in one

Blown-In Cellulose

Recycled paper fiber blown to a perfect density, filling every cavity. Class A fire-rated, eco-friendly, and dramatically quieter.

  • R-3.7 per inch
  • 85% recycled content
  • Reduces sound transfer 60%+

Fiberglass Batt

Cost-effective rolled or batt insulation for new construction, garages, and renovations. Fast install, predictable R-value.

  • R-13 / R-19 / R-30 stock
  • Faced or unfaced options
  • Best price-per-square-foot

Attic Insulation

FAQ

Common questions, honest answers.

How much does insulation cost?

Most attic projects run $1.50–$4.50 per sq ft installed depending on material and R-value. Spray foam runs higher ($3–$7/sq ft) but pays back in 2–4 years through energy savings. Your matched contractors give itemized written quotes — no surprises.

Is the quote really free?

Yes. Contractors pay us a small referral fee only when a homeowner books — you pay $0 to compare quotes or get a site assessment.

What rebates and tax credits qualify?

The federal Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit (25C) covers 30% of insulation costs up to $1,200/year. Most utilities add another $200–$1,500 in rebates. Our contractors file the paperwork for you.

How long does install take?

Attic blow-ins: 2–4 hours. Whole-house spray foam: 1–2 days. Crawl space encapsulation: 1–3 days. Most projects do not require you to leave the home.

What if I'm not happy with the contractor?

Every match is backed by our 30-day satisfaction guarantee. If a job isn't right, we'll connect you with another vetted pro to make it right — at no extra cost.
